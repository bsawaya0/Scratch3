Last Season Recap

BYU finished the 2024 season as an Alamo Bowl champion and a top‑15 team, showing it can compete at a high level in the Big 12.

The Cougars went 11‑2 overall and 7‑2 in conference play, beating Colorado 36‑14 in the Alamo Bowl and building serious momentum for the future.

Key 2024 Storylines

  1. Big 12 Progress
    • Improved conference record compared to the 2023 season.
    • Signature wins over teams like Baylor and Arizona.
  2. Bowl Game Success
    • Alamo Bowl victory over Colorado.
    • National rankings in both AP and Coaches polls.
  3. Defensive Growth
    • Better balance between offense and defense than in 2023.
    • Improved depth at key positions.

Bear Bachmeier: The New QB

Bear Bachmeier steps into the BYU offense as a dynamic, confident quarterback with the arm talent and mobility to push the ball downfield and stress Big 12 defenses.

Early highlights show his ability to make tight‑window throws, extend plays with his legs, and finish drives in the red zone, giving BYU a true modern dual‑threat at quarterback.

Why Bear Changes the Ceiling

  • Arm strength that fits BYU’s traditional vertical passing identity.
  • Mobility that forces defenses to account for quarterback runs and scrambles.
  • Composure in big moments, especially in conference play.

Future of the BYU Program

With a recent 11‑win season and a Big 12 title push, BYU has proven it can contend on a national stage while recruiting to a unique culture and home‑field environment.

Adding playmakers like Bear Bachmeier, continued Big 12 exposure, and strong facilities in Provo give the Cougars a realistic path to more New Year’s Six bowls and playoff conversations.

Program Priorities Going Forward

  • Offensive Consistency
    1. Stay explosive in the passing game.
    2. Develop a reliable run game to support the QB.
  • Recruiting and Development
    1. Win battles for Big 12‑level skill players.
    2. Build depth on both lines of scrimmage.
  • National Relevance
    1. Schedule and win high‑profile non‑conference games.
    2. Stay ranked and make regular bowl appearances.
